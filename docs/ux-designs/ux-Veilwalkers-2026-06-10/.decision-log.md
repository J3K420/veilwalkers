## 2026-06-10 — Brain dump (James, verbatim intent)

- **Core tone principle:** "Spooky but fun should be the vibe — but the monsters can be very scary."
- **Interpretation (to confirm):** The *frame* (UI chrome, menus, Home/Codex/Shop, microcopy, moment-to-moment feel) is playful and inviting. The *monsters themselves* + the AR encounter are allowed to be genuinely scary. The contrast is intentional and load-bearing — friendly wrapper makes the scary payload hit harder (haunted-house-ride model). Teen-rated ceiling still applies (stylized scary, no realistic gore per CLAUDE.md / content rating).
- **Design implication:** Fun lives in system surfaces; dread lives in the AR camera moment + monster art. The tension is the brand.

## 2026-06-10 — Brain dump round 2 (James, verbatim intent)

- **UI frame tone locked:** Saturday-morning-cartoon. Bright, playful, inviting — the constant chrome.
- **Monster scariness is a SPECTRUM, not a setting:** ranges from "cartoon kid scary" (almost playful) → "terrifying 3D pus-covered monstrosities." Both ends ship.
- **Progression = tone arc (the adventure element):** Player STARTS luring/catching the almost-playful monsters and BUILDS UP to big scary monsters. The difficulty/progression curve is expressed as escalating dread, not just stats. Early game = friendly; late game = nightmare.
- **Materialization variety, tiered to scariness:** There is no single AR-entry animation. Materializations VARY and each relates to the scariness of the monster it delivers — gentle/playful entrances for cartoon monsters, dread-heavy/uncanny entrances for terrifying ones. Materialization tier scales with monster tier.
- **Hero moment (universal):** "The ohhh-cool moments should come from the monsters entering the environment." The materialization INTO the player's real space is THE signature moment of the product. This makes the AR-entry animation the single most important interaction primitive — and it is tiered.

### Derived structuring decisions (to confirm with James)
- Monster art + materializations organized into **tiers** (e.g. Tier 1 playful → Tier N terrifying). Exact tier count TBD; design doc / PRD progression may already imply it.
- DESIGN.md will need to express the **constant cartoon UI identity** AND a **tiered "dread scale"** for encounter surfaces (lighting, motion, color shift as scariness rises).
- EXPERIENCE.md will need a **Materialization interaction primitive** spec'd per tier (timing, motion, audio cue, camera behavior) + the climax beat in Key Flows = a high-tier materialization.

## 2026-06-10 — Encounter flow + materialization decisions

**Encounter loop skeleton (proposed, accepted as working structure):**
Enter AR Hunt → AR Safety card → camera live + plane-coaching → raise phone ("part the Veil") → planes detected → LURE (Basic 1 / Premium 4 / Multi 5, cost shown before spend) → ★ MATERIALIZATION (tiered) ★ → ENCOUNTER ACTIVE (monster anchored, ambiance shifted to dread tier; action bar SCAN/CAPTURE/SLAY + charge-items) → SCAN (free, reveals + Codex progress) / CAPTURE (free base or Strong-Capture charge → Codex reveal) / SLAY (3cr, red, tap-to-slay → loot) → resolve → Codex reveal if new → re-lure or exit.

**DECISION — Action bar during materialization: HIDDEN until it lands.** Materialization plays clean & cinematic, no buttons; action bar animates/pops in when monster settles. Protects the hero moment from UI clutter.

**DECISION — Entrance length scales with tier.** T1 ~0.5s snappy pop → T5 ~2-3s dramatic breach. Entrance length is itself part of the reward; rare monsters feel like events. (Note for EXPERIENCE.md: must still honor NFR-2 sub-second ACK of the credit SPEND — the lure deduction confirms instantly even while the materialization animation plays out longer. Spend-ack ≠ materialization duration.)

## 2026-06-10 — DECISION: 5 Materializations LOCKED (the hero primitive)

James chose "Love them, lock all 5" incl. the T5 UI-glitch. This is THE signature interaction primitive → EXPERIENCE.md Materialization section (5 variants) + climax beat in Key Flows. Spec'd on axes: motion / ambiance (lighting+tint) / audio / camera / duration.

**T1 · Common · "Pop-in" · ~0.5s** — Motion: candy-teal mist poof, squash-stretch overshoot, wobble-settle. Ambiance: none, full bright friendly. Audio: cartoon *bamf* + boing. Camera: static. Feel: "aw, hello little guy."

**T2 · Uncommon · "Unfurl" · ~1s** — Motion: translucent veil-ribbons peel back from center, monster fades up through shimmer, curious twitch. Ambiance: faint teal→periwinkle vignette breathes in, holds soft. Audio: soft chime-shimmer + one low note. Camera: 1-2% breathing zoom, no shake. Feel: "ooh, what's this."

**T3 · Rare · "Seep" · ~1.5s** — Motion: dark pool spreads on surface, monster rises out head-first, eyes catch light before body resolves. Ambiance: lights visibly DIM, overlay cools toward `#6A5AC9`, real vignette closes, cartoon brightness drains. Audio: rising drone + wet shadow-rustle → silence as it locks eyes. Camera: slow push-in + one soft pulse. Feel: "okay, this one's looking at me."

**T4 · Epic · "Tear" · ~2s** — Motion: jagged rip tears open in the air above plane, world warps/refracts around gash, monster forces through (too big for hole), drops with weight. Ambiance: heavy `#4B2E6B` tint, strong vignette, chromatic-aberration at tear edges. Audio: sub-bass swell + tearing crunch + landing thud. Camera: real shake on tear + landing, brief push-in. Feel: "oh no. oh no."

**T5 · Nightmare · "Breach" · ~2.5-3s** — Motion: the CAMERA FEED ITSELF "corrupts" — screen edges crawl with glitching veil-rot, picture distorts; monstrosity erupts claiming whole space; final lunge-toward-camera beat before settle. Ambiance: full bruise-rot grade (`#3A4C3A → #5C2A4A`), oppressive vignette, AND the friendly cartoon UI momentarily flickers/glitches as if the Nightmare interferes with the app itself. Audio: silence → hard audio sting + guttural roar + chest-felt low end. Camera: strong shake, snap push-in on lunge, settle. Feel: THE hero "ohhh cool / oh god" moment.

**Throughlines (preserve in spine):**
1. T5 UI-glitch = the "fun frame vs scary monster" thesis made literal — Nightmare briefly BREAKS the cartoon wrapper. Signature beat. (Risk note for EXPERIENCE.md/Accessibility: must be clearly a designed FX, not read as a crash; keep brief, branded glitch styling, never block input recovery; respect a reduced-motion / photosensitivity setting — see a11y floor.)
2. All 5 reuse the architecture's "veil" metaphor (lure VFX = veil-parting; lost-anchor restore reuses materialization VFX). Consistent, not bolted on.
3. Action bar HIDDEN during all 5, pops in on settle. Duration scales T1→T5. Spend-ack stays sub-second regardless.

## 2026-06-10 — DECISION: Codex = Hybrid gradual silhouette reveal (RESOLVES OQ-7)

James chose the **hybrid**. Codex presentation:
- **All 67 slots always present** (the "67" goal is viscerally felt — grid shows full scope as count "X / 67").
- **Undiscovered monsters in tiers the player has BEGUN encountering** = locked **`???` silhouette** (you see the shape you're missing).
- **Undiscovered monsters in the top unearned tiers (esp. Nightmare/Epic the player hasn't reached)** = fully hidden **blank `?` slots, no silhouette** — preserves the scary reveals and front-loads nothing. Scope + dread reveal GRADUALLY, mirroring the playful→terrifying ramp.
- **The Codex is itself a dread-ramp instrument:** as the player climbs tiers, more silhouettes "unlock into view," so the collection's own scope escalates with the arc.
- **Implication for tier-gating:** need a rule for WHEN a tier's silhouettes become visible (proposal: a tier's silhouettes appear once the player has encountered/discovered their first monster of that tier, OR reached a progress threshold — to confirm/feed upstream; intersects OQ-2 rarity model + progression).
- Per-monster detail page: art, name, rarity-tier badge (color-coded to dread scale), wry-eerie lore entry, capture/slay stats, first-discovered date. Locked entries show silhouette/blank + "Not yet discovered" + (for encountered-tier) a teaser hint.
- **Codex reveal animation** (on first discovery, fired from encounter resolve): slot flips/illuminates from silhouette→full art with a satisfying stamp/"caught" beat in the tier's color; count ticks up "11/67 → 12/67". For a newly-unlocked TIER, an extra beat: new silhouettes fade into the grid ("the Veil shows you more...").
- Supersedes architecture `[ASSUMPTION]` that Codex shows all 67 silhouettes — refined to gradual/tiered. Feed upstream.
