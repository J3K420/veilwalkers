# Story 3.2: Gate AR entry behind the mandatory Safety Warning

Status: done

## Story

As a player,
I want a safety warning every time I enter AR,
So that I stay aware of my surroundings (a Google Play requirement).

## Acceptance Criteria

**AC-1 (mandatory gate — blocks the camera view and every Encounter action until acknowledged).**
**Given** a cold entry into AR Mode
**When** the player enters
**Then** the AR Safety Warning (`ArSafetyGate`) appears and the camera view is not interactive until acknowledged
**And** no Lure or Encounter action is reachable until acknowledgement
**And** safety copy is plain, high-contrast, and never buried in flavor (cannot be A/B'd away).
[Source: docs/epics.md#Story-3.2; docs/architecture.md:482-486 (Safety-warning entry rule); FR-3]

**AC-2 (full read on first entry / first-per-session; fast on-brand card on later cold entries).**
**Given** the first entry (and first per session)
**When** the warning shows
**Then** it is a deliberate full read; later cold entries show it as a fast on-brand card (mandatory presence, minimal dwell).
[Source: docs/epics.md#Story-3.2; docs/architecture.md:485-486]

**AC-3 (cold-entry rule only — Shop round-trip resume does NOT re-fire the full warning).**
**Given** a resume after a Shop round-trip
**When** AR resumes
**Then** the full warning does NOT re-fire (cold-entry rule only).
[Source: docs/epics.md#Story-3.2; docs/architecture.md:482-484, 525; AR-9]

### The design decisions this story locks (call them out for the CR reviewer)
1. **`ArEntryKind` is a caller-supplied INPUT, not a gate-inferred fact.** The gate cannot know whether an entry is a cold entry or a Shop resume — that classification lives upstream in the navigation/session context (the AppStateMachine, Story 6.3 / `ArSessionService`, Story 3.3). So the gate takes `ArEntryKind` as a parameter to `RequestEntry`, exactly as `CameraPermissionFlow.Evaluate()` takes OS truth from the `ICameraPermission` seam rather than guessing. This keeps the gate a pure, deterministically-testable decision and puts the "is this cold?" knowledge where it actually lives. (Architecture.md:482-484 makes the cold-entry rule a navigation-context rule, not a gate-internal heuristic.)
2. **The gate IS branching logic — and that does NOT violate AR's "thin adapter, zero branching logic" mandate.** This is the identical reconciliation Story 3.1 made for `CameraPermissionFlow` (decision #2 there). architecture.md:466 ("AR contains no branching logic worth testing — thin adapter") targets the OS-TOUCHING adapters (`AndroidCameraPermission`, the future `ArSessionService` ARCore glue). The full/fast/skip/acknowledge STATE MACHINE is precisely branching worth testing, isolated in the pure-logic `ArSafetyGate` (no Unity types, headless-tested). There is NO OS-touching adapter in 3.2 at all — the gate wraps nothing platform-specific (it is pure session state), so the mandate is satisfied trivially. Document so the CR does not flag the gate as an AR-mandate violation.
3. **`MayInteract` is the SINGLE interaction-block predicate (AC-1).** AC-1's "camera view is not interactive AND no Lure/Encounter action is reachable until acknowledged" is enforced by one readable boolean (`true` only in `Acknowledged`), not by scattered `state == X` checks in consumers. The consumers (AR-HUD, EncounterService — Epic 4/6) read this ONE predicate. This keeps the block in one provable place and makes "cannot be A/B'd away" structural: the fast card is ALSO `MayInteract == false` (mandatory presence — a fast card is faster, never skippable). Pin it with the `MayInteract`-in-every-non-acknowledged-state test.
4. **Session-scoped state only — NO persistence in 3.2 (deliberate scope call).** AC-2 says "first entry (and first per session)" gets the full read; "later cold entries" get the fast card. I read "first per session" as **first cold entry per APP session** — the gate is a registered singleton that lives for the app's process lifetime, holds an in-memory "shown this session" latch, and a fresh app launch = a fresh full read. This satisfies AC-2 deterministically with ZERO `SaveService`/`IClock` dependency. A persisted "has the player EVER seen the full warning" (so a returning player on a NEW session could get a fast card on their very first cold entry) is a possible refinement, but: (a) the AC text's "first per session" most naturally means per-session, and (b) erring toward the FULL read on every new app session is the SAFE direction for a mandatory Google-Play safety gate (showing the deliberate read more often is never a Play-compliance risk; showing it less could be). So 3.2 stays session-scoped; the persisted-first-ever refinement is recorded as a deferral (Epic 6 onboarding / a balancing decision), NOT built. Call this out for the CR so the absence of persistence is read as a sanctioned scope decision, not an omission.
5. **`Acknowledged` is a hand-off signal, not an AR start (mirrors 3.1's `Granted`).** On `Acknowledged`, the gate signals "the warning is satisfied — AR may proceed." It does NOT call `ArSessionService` (3.3), prewarm, or touch the camera. The caller (3.3/6.3) sequences permission → safety gate → session. Keeps the story scoped to the warning gate only.

### Edge cases & boundary rules (must-handle — these become test cases)
- **First cold entry of the session:** `RequestEntry(ColdEntry)` → `BlockingFull`, `MayInteract == false` (full deliberate read, AC-2).
- **Acknowledge the full read:** `Acknowledge()` → `Acknowledged`, `MayInteract == true` (camera + Encounter released, AC-1).
- **Second cold entry of the session:** `RequestEntry(ColdEntry)` → `BlockingFastCard`, `MayInteract == false` (fast card, STILL mandatory-blocking — cannot be A/B'd away, AC-1+AC-2).
- **Shop resume on a fresh gate:** `RequestEntry(ShopResume)` → `Acknowledged` directly, `MayInteract == true`, warning does NOT fire (AC-3); AND the session-shown latch is NOT burned — a later first `ColdEntry` still → `BlockingFull` (resume is not a "read").
- **Shop resume after a cold full read:** cold full → ack → `RequestEntry(ShopResume)` → `Acknowledged`, `MayInteract == true` (resume transparent regardless of prior state, AC-3).
- **Illegal `Acknowledge` from `Inactive`:** `GameLog.Warn` + stays `Inactive`, `MayInteract == false`, no throw (NFR-3).
- **Illegal/redundant `Acknowledge` from `Acknowledged`:** `GameLog.Warn` + stays `Acknowledged`, `MayInteract == true`, no double-fire (idempotent-safe).
- **`MayInteract` is false in EVERY non-`Acknowledged` state** (`Inactive`, `BlockingFull`, `BlockingFastCard`) and true ONLY in `Acknowledged` — the single AC-1 block (mutation-testable).
- **View inert (gate unregistered / services not ready):** `EnterArMode`/`OnAcknowledge` no-op + `GameLog.Warn`, never throw at `Awake` (the 2.4/2.5/3.1 graceful-degrade precedent).
